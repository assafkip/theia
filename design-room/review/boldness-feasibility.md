# Floor — boldness-feasibility (FORK — the ONE bold moment vs scan-speed + LCP)

Von Restorff: one pattern-breaking moment earns memory; a second one cancels the
first. Where does KTLYST Extract spend its ONE bold move? (fork)

- **A — the DROPPED-COUNT as the bold moment.** A large, unmissable "N facts dropped"
  element — the thing no competitor shows. Highest differentiation, on-spine.
- **B — the GROUNDING ACT animated (DURING state).** Facts appear, then visibly
  snap to their source-span (or dissolve if ungrounded). The mechanic made visible.
  Bold + explains the product in one motion. Higher build cost.
- **C — the RECEIPT/source-span as a bespoke inline artifact** (the AFTER state hero):
  every fact tethered to its verbatim quote with a made-for-this-page connector.

feasibility note: all three are cheap on LCP if the hero is type/HTML (both references
are HTML-hero, no heavy WebGL). B costs the most JS but is client-side and post-upload
(off the LCP path). fork: founder picks where the boldness goes. The tool may not pick.
